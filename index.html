Research Interests

My research is focused on the structure of protoplanetary disks using the MATISSE (Multi-Aperture mid-Infrared SpectroScopic Experiment) instrument.
I am particularly interested in the inner regions of the circumstellar disks (i.e., the region within a few astronomical units of the central star) where it is most likely for terrestrial planets to form.

I am currently working on the HD 142666 system, a Herbig Ae/Be star with a multi-ringed inner disk. The aim of my research is to understand the asymmetries in the structure of the inner disk and their implications for the formation of planets.

My work involves the analysis of interferometric observations in the mid-infrared wavelength range (H, K, L and M bands), and also includes the modeling of the disk structure using (simplified) radiative transfer codes (temperature gradients).

I am working on this under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Dr. Thomas Henning and Dr. Roy van Boekel at the Max-Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA), Heidelberg, Germany

Work projects (My Affiliation)

• oimodeler (Contributor):

A modular modelling tool for optical interferometry writen among others by members of the MATISSE consortium in order to present an easy to use tool for the whole of the optical and generally the interferometric community.

• ppdmod (Owner):

An interferometric modelling tool, which I am step by step integrating into oimodeler (it will then be archived).

• matadrs (Owner):

The (modular) MATISSE Automated Data Reduction Software (Matadrs) is a tool written (with some code adapted from colleagues) to encompass all the individual steps and files needed to procure and reduce data generally and easily in as few steps as possible with the least knowledge required. To make it possible for seasoned MATISSE users to quickly and easily reduce data and giving others who are interested in MATISSE data the opportunity to do so as well.

• p2obt (Owner):

The Phase 2 OB preparation tool (p2obt) is made to streamline/automate the process of MATISSE observation preparation on p2 (can theoretically also be adapted for all other VLTI instruments, if templates are added).
