Research Interests

My research is focused on the structure of protoplanetary discs using the MATISSE (Multi-Aperture mid-Infrared SpectroScopic Experiment) instrument.
I am particularly interested in the inner regions of the circumstellar disks (i.e. from <1 au to a few astronomical units) where it is most likely for terrestrial planets to form.
The aim of my research is to understand the structure and composition of these inner discs and their implications for planet formation.
I do this by analysising interferometric data over multiple wavelength bands (H, K, L, M, and N) via Bayesian model fitting with (simple) radiative transfer codes (analytical temperature gradient).

Code (My Affiliation)

• oimodeler (Contributor):

A modular modelling tool for optical interferometry writen among others by members of the MATISSE consortium in order to present an easy to use tool for the whole of the optical and generally the interferometric community.

• p2obt (Owner):

The Phase 2 OB preparation tool (p2obt) is made to streamline/automate the process of MATISSE observation preparation on p2 (can theoretically also be adapted for all other VLTI instruments, if templates are added).

• ppdmod (Owner):

An interferometric modelling tool, which I am step by step integrating into oimodeler (it will then be archived).

• Fourim (Owner):

(Work in progress) Fourim is an interactive tool to visualise the amplitudes and phases seen during interferometric observations.
